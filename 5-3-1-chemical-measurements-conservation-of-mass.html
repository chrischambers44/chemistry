Core ideas for 5.3.1

Conservation of mass means: in a chemical reaction, atoms are not created or destroyed. So, in a closed system, the total mass of reactants = total mass of products.

Balanced symbol equations (5.3.1.1)

  • Equations must have the same number of each atom on both sides.
  • You can change coefficients (big numbers in front), but never change subscripts.
  • Balanced equations show conservation of mass at the atom level.
Example:
Unbalanced: H₂ + O₂ → H₂O
Balanced: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

Relative formula mass Mr (5.3.1.2)

  • Mr is the sum of relative atomic masses (Ar) in the formula.
  • Use it to compare masses of different substances, and as a step toward calculations later (5.3.2).
Element Typical Ar Element Typical Ar
H1C12
N14O16
Na23Mg24
Al27Cl35.5
Ca40S32
Example: Mr(H₂O) = 2×1 + 16 = 18

Mass changes when gases are involved (5.3.1.3)

In many school experiments, the mass seems to change because the system is open. If a gas escapes, the measured mass can decrease. If a gas from the air is taken in, the mass can increase.
What happens? Typical observation Best explanation
Gas is produced and escapes (open flask) Mass decreases Gas particles leave the apparatus, so the balance reads less
Gas from air is used up (e.g. oxidation) Mass increases Oxygen from air is added to the solid, increasing mass
Closed system (bung/balloon/sealed container) Mass stays the same No particles can leave or enter; total mass is conserved

Exam tip: “Why did the mass change?”
If the question mentions an open container, the simplest explanation is usually: a gas escaped (mass decreases) or oxygen from the air was gained (mass increases). If it’s a sealed container, mass should stay the same.

Common mark-scheme phrases (copy-friendly)

Use these in “explain” questions (especially mass-change practical-style questions).

Conservation of mass

“In a closed system, the mass of the products equals the mass of the reactants.”
“No atoms are created or destroyed in a chemical reaction.”

Balanced equations

“The equation is balanced when there are the same numbers of each atom on both sides.”
“Only the coefficients can be changed; the subscripts in the formula must stay the same.”

Mr phrases

“Relative formula mass is the sum of the relative atomic masses in the formula.”

Mass change with gases

“The mass decreased because a gas was produced and escaped from the apparatus.”
“The mass increased because oxygen from the air reacted with the substance.”
Tip: If the word “open” is in the question, say “gas escaped” (or “oxygen gained”) and you usually score.
