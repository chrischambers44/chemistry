AQA 5.1.1.1 – Atoms, elements, compounds and mixtures

Foundation concept for all chemistry: what matter is made of, and how substances are classified.

Core knowledge

All substances are made of atoms. How atoms are arranged and combined determines whether a substance is an element, compound, or mixture.

Atom

  • Smallest particle of an element
  • Cannot be broken down by chemical reactions
  • All matter is made of atoms

Element

  • Substance made of only one type of atom
  • Cannot be chemically broken down
  • Examples: oxygen (O₂), iron (Fe), carbon (C)

Compound

  • Two or more different elements chemically bonded
  • Fixed ratio of atoms
  • Can be chemically broken down
  • Example: water (H₂O), sodium chloride (NaCl)

Mixture

  • Two or more substances not chemically bonded
  • No fixed ratio
  • Can be separated by physical methods
  • Example: air, seawater
