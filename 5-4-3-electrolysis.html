AQA 5.4.3 Tutorial – Electrolysis

This lesson covers electrolysis: what it is, how ions move, what happens at the cathode and anode, predicting products (molten vs aqueous), and why electrolysis is used to extract some metals.

Core idea for 5.4.3

Electrolysis is the decomposition of an ionic compound using electricity. It only works when ions can move (in a molten ionic compound or an aqueous solution).

5.4.3.1 The process of electrolysis

  • Needs electrodes and an electrolyte (molten or aqueous ionic compound).
  • Positive ions (cations) move to the cathode (–).
  • Negative ions (anions) move to the anode (+).
  • Reduction happens at the cathode (gain of electrons).
  • Oxidation happens at the anode (loss of electrons).
Handy memory: OIL RIG (Oxidation Is Loss, Reduction Is Gain) + Cathode is negative.
Quick check (movement)
During electrolysis, cations move to the…
Explanation

5.4.3.2 Electrolysis of molten ionic compounds

  • Only the ions from the compound are present (no water ions).
  • At the cathode: metal ions are reduced to the metal.
  • At the anode: non-metal ions form non-metals (often gases).
  • Example (molten lead bromide): Pb²⁺ → Pb, and 2Br⁻ → Br₂
Quick check (molten)
In molten lead bromide, the product at the cathode is…
Explanation

Electrolysis of aqueous solutions (foundation-friendly rules)

  • Water adds extra ions: H⁺ and OH⁻.
  • At the cathode: if the metal is more reactive than hydrogen, hydrogen gas is produced; otherwise the metal is produced.
  • At the anode: if halide ions (Cl⁻, Br⁻, I⁻) are present, halogen is produced; otherwise oxygen is produced from OH⁻.
AQA often uses examples like aqueous copper sulfate and brine (NaCl).
Quick check (aqueous)
In aqueous copper sulfate with inert electrodes, what forms at the cathode?
Explanation

5.4.3.3 Using electrolysis to extract metals

  • Very reactive metals (above carbon) can’t be extracted by carbon reduction.
  • They are extracted by electrolysis of molten compounds (e.g. aluminium from aluminium oxide).
  • Electrolysis uses a lot of energy (electricity cost is important).
Quick check (extraction)
Aluminium is extracted by electrolysis because…
Explanation
Key vocabulary
Electrolyte – molten or dissolved ionic compound containing mobile ions.
Electrode – conductor where ions gain/lose electrons (cathode/anode).
Cathode – negative electrode; reduction happens here.
Anode – positive electrode; oxidation happens here.
Inert electrode – does not react (e.g. graphite/platinum).
Decomposition – breaking down a compound into simpler substances/elements.
