Core ideas for 5.1.1.3

The atomic model changed because new experimental evidence didn’t fit the old model. GCSE questions often test the sequence of models and the conclusions from the alpha scattering experiment.
Dalton (early 1800s)
Atoms are tiny solid spheres. Each element is made of one type of atom.
Thomson (1897)
Discovery of the electron → “plum pudding model”: a ball of positive charge with negative electrons embedded.
Rutherford (1909–1911)
Alpha particle scattering showed the atom has a small, dense, positively charged nucleus and is mostly empty space.
Bohr (1913)
Electrons orbit the nucleus in fixed energy levels (shells). This explained why atoms are stable.
Later development (modern model)
Evidence led to the idea that electrons exist in regions called orbitals (beyond GCSE detail).

Alpha scattering: what happened?

Scientists fired alpha particles at a thin sheet of gold foil. They observed:
  • Most went straight through
  • Some were deflected (changed direction)
  • A tiny number bounced back
Why is this surprising?
If the plum pudding model were correct (positive charge spread out), alpha particles should pass through with only small deflections. Large deflections and bouncing back suggested a concentrated positive centre.

Alpha scattering: conclusions (the mark points)

  • Most particles passed through → the atom is mostly empty space
  • Some deflected → there is a positive charge in the atom
  • Very few bounced back → the positive charge (and mass) is in a tiny, dense nucleus
Exam-safe wording: “A small, dense, positively charged nucleus” + “mostly empty space”.
Common confusion: what did Bohr add?
Rutherford’s model had a nucleus, but electrons orbiting like planets didn’t explain why electrons don’t spiral into the nucleus. Bohr proposed electrons are in fixed energy levels.

Alpha scattering conclusions

“Some alpha particles were deflected, so the nucleus is positively charged.”
“A few alpha particles bounced back, so the nucleus is small, dense and contains most of the mass.”

Model changes

“The atomic model changed because new experimental evidence did not fit the previous model.”
“Bohr proposed electrons orbit the nucleus in fixed energy levels (shells).”

Named models (short descriptions)

“Thomson’s plum pudding model had electrons embedded in a sphere of positive charge.”
