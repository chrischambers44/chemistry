Core ideas for 5.1.3

Carbon has 4 outer electrons, so it forms 4 covalent bonds. Carbon atoms can bond to each other, forming chains and rings. This is why there are so many carbon compounds.

Carbon bonding (why it forms many compounds)

  • Carbon is in Group 4 → 4 outer electrons
  • It forms 4 covalent bonds to get a full outer shell
  • Carbon can form single bonds (C–C) and (later, organic) double bonds
  • Carbon atoms can form chains and rings
Example: methane is CH₄ because carbon forms 4 single covalent bonds.

Allotropes of carbon

An allotrope is a different form of the same element, with atoms arranged differently. Different structures → different properties.
Allotrope Structure Key properties (GCSE)
Diamond Each carbon bonds to 4 others (giant covalent) Very hard, very high melting point, does not conduct
Graphite Each carbon bonds to 3 others in layers; 1 electron per C is delocalised Conducts, soft/slippery (layers slide), high melting point
Graphene (HT) Single layer of graphite (one-atom thick sheet) Very strong, conducts, flexible, huge surface area
Fullerenes (HT) Carbon arranged in hollow shapes (spheres/tubes) Useful for drug delivery, catalysts, lubricants, electronics (examples)

Diamond vs graphite: “structure → property” links

Diamond: 4 covalent bonds per carbon → a rigid 3D network → very hard.
No delocalised electrons → does not conduct electricity.
Graphite: 3 covalent bonds per carbon → layered structure.
Layers held by weak forces → layers can slide → soft / slippery.
One delocalised electron per carbon → conducts electricity.

Simple “exam sketches” (words-only)

You don’t need to draw perfect hexagons, but you must describe the key features.

Exam tip: the “magic words” for carbon allotropes
Diamond: 4 bonds, giant covalent, hard, no delocalised electrons (no conduction).
Graphite: 3 bonds, layers, weak forces between layers, delocalised electrons (conducts).
Graphene (HT): single layer, strong, conducts, flexible, high surface area.

Common mark-scheme phrases (copy-friendly)

These are the exact kind of sentences that score marks in “explain why” questions.

Allotropes

“An allotrope is a different form of the same element with a different arrangement of atoms.”

Diamond

“In diamond, each carbon atom forms four covalent bonds in a giant covalent structure, making it very hard.”
“Diamond does not conduct because it has no delocalised electrons.”

Graphite

“Graphite has layers; weak forces between layers allow them to slide, so graphite is soft/slippery.”
“Graphite conducts because it has delocalised electrons that can move through the structure.”

Graphene / fullerenes (HT)

“Graphene is a single layer of graphite; it is very strong and conducts electricity.”
“Fullerenes are carbon molecules with hollow shapes; they have potential uses such as drug delivery and catalysts.”
Tip: If you ever say “strong covalent bonds” + “giant covalent structure” + “delocalised electrons”, you are usually on the right track.
