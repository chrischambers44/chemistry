5.4.3 Supplementary Quiz – Diatomic Elements

This short module supports 5.4.3 Electrolysis by reinforcing diatomic elements and why products like Cl₂ form.

Why this matters

In electrolysis questions, AQA expects you to already know that some elements exist as diatomic molecules. This is assumed knowledge, not retaught in 5.4.3.
  • Writing Cl instead of Cl₂ can lose marks
  • You are not asked why they are diatomic — only to recognise them

The diatomic elements you must remember

If these elements are written on their own, they are always diatomic:
Have No Fear Of Ice Cold Beer
  • H₂ – Hydrogen
  • N₂ – Nitrogen
  • F₂ – Fluorine
  • O₂ – Oxygen
  • I₂ – Iodine
  • Cl₂ – Chlorine
  • Br₂ – Bromine
