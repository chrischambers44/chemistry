5.4.3 Supplementary Quiz – Diatomic Elements

This short module supports 5.4.3 Electrolysis by reinforcing diatomic elements and why products like Cl₂ form at the anode.

Why this matters

In electrolysis questions, AQA expects you to already know that some elements exist naturally as diatomic molecules (pairs of atoms). This is assumed knowledge, not usually re-taught in 5.4.3.
  • Writing Cl instead of Cl₂ in an equation can lose marks
  • You’re rarely asked to explain why they’re diatomic — you mainly need to remember the formulas

The diatomic elements to remember

If these elements are written on their own in equations, they are diatomic:

  • H₂ – Hydrogen
  • N₂ – Nitrogen
  • F₂ – Fluorine
  • O₂ – Oxygen
  • I₂ – Iodine
  • Cl₂ – Chlorine
  • Br₂ – Bromine
